Blinkit Sales Analysis | Business Insights and Revenue Optimization
Project Overview

This project demonstrates an end-to-end data analytics workflow where raw retail data is transformed into actionable insights using Power BI, DAX, and data modeling techniques.

The objective is to move beyond basic visualization and focus on analyzing business performance, identifying patterns, and supporting data-driven decision-making.

Key Highlights
Identified Tier 3 cities as major revenue contributors outperforming metro regions
Observed strong demand for low-fat products contributing significantly to total sales
Found that medium-sized outlets achieve the best balance between cost and revenue
Developed an interactive dashboard enabling multi-dimensional business analysis

This analysis reflects real-world retail challenges where businesses must continuously optimize performance across locations, products, and customer segments.

The project highlights how data can be used to identify growth opportunities, improve efficiency, and support strategic planning.

Problem Statement

Retail businesses often face challenges in:

Identifying key revenue drivers
Understanding customer purchase behavior
Optimizing outlet and product performance

This project addresses these challenges through structured analysis and interactive visualization.

Key Insights and Business Impact
Sales Trend Analysis

Sales peaked around 2018, followed by a decline and later stabilization. This suggests possible market saturation or increased competition over time.
To address this, I would focus on revisiting pricing strategies, introducing targeted promotions, and analyzing competitor positioning to regain growth momentum.

Geographic Performance

Tier 3 cities outperform Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities in terms of overall sales contribution. This indicates stronger demand and potentially lower competition in non-metro regions.
Based on this, I would prioritize expansion and marketing efforts in Tier 3 locations to capitalize on high-growth opportunities.

Consumer Behavior

Low-fat products contribute approximately 65% of total revenue, indicating a clear shift toward health-conscious purchasing behavior.
To leverage this trend, I would expand the range of health-focused products and align marketing strategies to highlight these offerings.

Product Performance

Fruits & Vegetables, Snack Foods, and Household Items emerge as the top-performing categories, reflecting high-frequency and essential purchases.
To maximize revenue, I would introduce bundling strategies and cross-selling techniques around these categories to increase average order value.

Outlet Optimization

Medium-sized outlets generate the highest sales, suggesting an optimal balance between operational cost and revenue generation.
Based on this, I would recommend replicating the medium outlet model in future expansions to maintain efficiency and scalability.

Business Recommendations
Expand operations in Tier 3 cities to capture higher demand and lower competition
Increase focus on health-oriented product categories
Implement bundling strategies to improve average transaction value
Scale medium-sized outlet formats for operational efficiency
Improve service quality in lower-rated outlets to enhance customer retention
